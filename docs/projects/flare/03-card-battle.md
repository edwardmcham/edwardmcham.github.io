# Core Rule: The Card Battle

When a player attempts to capture an opponent's piece, both players resolve a card battle before the capture happens. The outcome decides whether the capture succeeds.

## How to Play a Card Battle

1. Declare the capture. Move your piece to the target square.
2. Place 3 cards face-down, then 1 card face-up. Both players do this simultaneously.
3. Compare the face-up cards. The higher card wins.
4. Apply the outcome. See [Card Battle Outcomes](#card-battle-outcomes).

## Card Rankings

| Rank | Notes |
|---|---|
| Joker (Sniper) | Highest possible card. Trumps Aces. See [Sniper Rules](05-sniper-rules.md). |
| Ace | Second highest. Beats all numbered cards. |
| King through 2 | Standard descending rank. King (K) high, 2 low. |

## Card Battle Outcomes

| Result | What Happens | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Attacker wins | Capture succeeds. Attacker's piece moves to the target square. Defender's piece is removed. | Standard capture. |
| Defender wins | Attacker's piece returns to its original square. | Capture attempt fails. |
| Tie (no Jokers) | The attacking piece returns to its original square. The turn is consumed. Neither player loses a piece. | Re-attempt on a future turn. |
| Attacker wins with Joker | Capture succeeds. The attacker also takes one additional non-King piece of their choice. | See [Sniper Rules](05-sniper-rules.md). |
| Tie (both Jokers) | Armageddon. Both pieces are removed. Each player also removes three non-King pieces of their choice. | See [Armageddon](05-sniper-rules.md#armageddon--both-players-play-a-sniper). |
