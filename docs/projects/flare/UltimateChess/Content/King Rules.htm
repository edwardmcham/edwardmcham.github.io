King Rules

Ultimate Chess has no check or checkmate. Kings move freely, and a King can be captured.

Kings Can Move Freely

A King may move into any square, including a square the opponent attacks. Neither player must move a King out of attack, and no move is illegal because it exposes a King.

Capturing a King

Attacking a King requires winning a best-of-three card battle, not a single round.

  1. Play up to three card battle rounds.
  2. The first player to win two rounds captures the King and wins the game.
  3. If the battle ties 1–1 after two rounds, play a third and final round.

Note: Sniper rules still apply within King battles. A Sniper win in any single round counts as winning that round.

King Capturing a Non-King Piece

When a King attacks a non-King piece, it plays a standard single-round card battle.

Important: If the King loses the card battle, the King is captured, and the attacking player loses the game immediately. Moving a King into a capture attempt is a gamble.

Last Piece Standing

If a player has no non-King pieces remaining, Sniper rules change:

  • A Sniper win counts as a standard card battle win only. No bonus piece is taken.
  • Armageddon — both Snipers — also resolves as a standard tie with no bonus pieces.
